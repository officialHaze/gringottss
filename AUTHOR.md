## Why I Build Things

Most of my projects begin with one of the following thoughts:

- "This should exist."
- "This exists, but I don't like it."
- "I wonder if I can build it myself."
- "How hard could it possibly be?"

The last one is usually where the trouble starts.

Sometimes I build things for other people.

Most of the time I build things because I needed them and got annoyed enough to do something about it.

Necessity may be the mother of invention.

Mild irritation is the father.

## Always Learning

One thing software development teaches you very quickly is humility.

The moment you think you've figured everything out, a compiler, framework, operating system, dependency, browser, kernel, or configuration file politely reminds you that you have not.

Every answer creates new questions.

Every rabbit hole contains another rabbit hole.

The more I learn, the more I realize how much there is left to learn.

And strangely enough, that's my favorite part.
